Pineapple Bananna Nut Bread




Pineapple makes bread moist and gives a slightly different flavor versus using bananas only. This is a nutritious sweet treat that can be used as a breakfast pastry or dessert snack. My family loves it and is one of my favorites to make.

Ingredients:

    3 cups unbleached all-purpose flour
    1 teaspoon baking soda
    1 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
    3 eggs, beaten
    2 teaspoons vanilla extract
    2 cups mashed ripe bananas (approximately 3 medium)
    2 cups sugar
    1 teaspoon salt
    1 cup nuts
    1 1/2 cups vegetable oil
    1 (8 oz.) can crushed pineapple, undrained

Directions:

  1. Heat oven to 350 degrees.

  2. Grease and flour 2 (9"x5") loaf pans.

  3. Combine dry ingredients.

  4. Stir in nuts and set aside.

  5. Combine remaining ingredients.

  6. Add to dry mix, stirring just until moistened.

  7. Spoon into prepared pans.

  8. Bake 50 to 60 minutes (about 80 minutes if using glass loaf pans).




Whipping Cream Pound Cake





This recipe brings back memories of my early childhood in Mississippi. My grandmother made this cake and this is a dessert I have passed along to my daughter as well. The best part of the experience is licking the bowl and spoon after preparation just as I did as a child!!



Ingredients:

    3 cups sugar
    3 cups cake flour
    6 eggs
    2 sticks of butter
    2 teaspoons vanilla
    8 ounces whipping cream

    Directions:

  1. Cream sugar and butter.

  2. Add eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition.

  3. Add alternately, while blending, the whipping cream and flour.

  4. Add vanilla and continue to blend.

  5. Pour into greased and floured tube pan and bake at 300 degrees for 1-1/2 to 2 hours.




Peach Crisp



This is a family favorite during Thanksgiving time. Canned peaches can be substituted for the fresh variety.



Ingredients:

    5 pounds peaches, about 12, each cut into 8 wedges
    1/3 cup sugar
    3/4 cup plus 1 tablespoon flour
    1 1/4 cups light brown sugar
    1/4 teaspoon kosher salt
    3/4 cup (1 1/2 sticks) butter, chilled and cut into small pieces
    2 cups rolled oats
    1 cup almonds, roughly chopped

Directions:

  1. Heat oven to 350 degrees.

  2. In large bowl, toss peaches, sugar and 1 T. of flour.

  3. Transfer to a shallow 3 quart baking dish.

  4. In another bowl, combine brown sugar, salt and remaining 3/4 cup flour.

  5. Using your fingers, incorporate butter until coarse crumbs form.

  6. Mix in the oats and almonds.

  7. Squeeze topping into marble size clumps and sprinkle on the peaches.

  8. Bake until the top is golden and the fruit is tender, 45 to 55 minutes for fresh peaches; approximately 35 minutes if using the canned variety.
